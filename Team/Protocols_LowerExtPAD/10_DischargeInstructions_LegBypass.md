Healing your Foot Wound

Now that your leg has better blood flow, your
foot sore (wound) will be more likely to heal.
Still, some work needs to be done.
The most important job you can do is to
keep pressure off the healing area of your
foot. Ask your team of doctors for a
protective shoe and possibly a walker,
crutches, or wheelchair to keep pressure off
while your foot heals. Walk only with a sock
or wound dressing AND your protective shoe,
even inside your home.


Be Sweet to Your Feet... Forever!

After your foot sore has healed, continue to
care of your feet to avoid foot infections and
amputations:
- Check your feet every day for cuts,
blisters, red spots, swelling, and sore
toenails. Use a plastic mirror to see the
bottom of your foot if needed.
- Wash your feet every day in warm (but
not hot) water, and be sure to dry well
between the toes.
-  Rub a thin coat of skin lotion on the
tops and bottoms of your feet.
- Trim your toenails carefully and
straight across when needed. See your
podiatrist if you need help.
- Never walk barefoot, and wear
comfortable shoes that fit well and
protect your feet. Nerve damage can
cause loss of feeling. Look and feel inside
your shoes before putting them on. You
can ask your team about getting special
shoes.
- Plan a physical activity program with
your health care team.
- Take your shoes and socks off at every
check-up and have your doctor look at
