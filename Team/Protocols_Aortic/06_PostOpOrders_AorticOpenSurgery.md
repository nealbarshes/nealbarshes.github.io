## Preoperative Considerations
1. Hold any anti-platelet other than aspirin 7 days prior to surgery; hold warfarin 5 days prior, DOACs 2 days prior
2. Avoid overnight fasting-- No solid food x 6hrs prior to surgery; clear liquid diet until 2 hours prior
3. Plan for epidural analgesia as part of a multi-modal anti-nociceptive protocol
4. Pre-operative Labs: CBC, Basic metabolic panel, PT/PTT/INR and B-type natriuretic peptide (BNP)  
5. Half of the typical home dose basal insulin (glargine) given in pre-operative area  


## Intraoperative Considerations
1. Cell saver  
2. First and second time-out prior to incision; third time-out just before cross-clamping  
•	Attending anesthesiologist in the OR  
•	Perfusionist in the OR  
•	Aortic graft on the table and ready for the surgical team  
•	48” 3-0 prolenes on the table and ready for the surgical team  
-Renal protective strategy  
•	Mannitol 12.5g/L IV	  
•	Cold renal perfusate: Mannitol 12.5g/L + Methylprednisolone 125mg/L + 1L lactated ringers @ 4 deg Celsius  
	
## Postoperative Orders: POD0
SICU admission with "SICU Mandatory Orders" order set  

### Fluid management  
1. Confirm amount of IVF given intraoperatively by anesthesia team to estimate fluid balance and to guide ongoing resuscitation/diuresis
2. Normal saline or lactated ringers 75cc/hr x 12hr (no more than 1.5L/day)
3. FFP and/or pRBC if uop < 0.5cc/hr or hypotension (MAP < 65)
4. Foley and strict I’s & Os at least q2h x 24hr  

### Diet & Bowel Regimen, at discretion of attending surgeon  
Considerations:  
1. Timing of nasogastric tube removal
2. Timing of clear liquid diet  

### Medications
1. T6-9 epidural analgesia  
2. the [team's multimodal pain regimen](https://github.com/nealbarshes/nealbarshes.github.io/blob/main/Team/Protocols_AllPatients/07_PostOpOrders_AllPatients/index.md#debakey-vascular-multimodal-pain-regimen)  
3. Glycemic control, applicable for BOTH diabetic and non-diabetic patient
4. Sliding scale to maintain glucose level < 180mg/dL
5. VTE Prophylaxis
6. Heparin subcutaneous q8h, per attending discretion
7. Antimicrobial coverage: 23hr perioperative antibiotics  

#### Laboratory Evaluation:  
1. CBC, BMP, PT/PTT/INR immediately post-op

## Discharge Instructions:
Resume previous diet  
Continue aspirin 81mg and statin daily  
OK to shower but do not submerge wound(s) under water (i.e. bathtub, swimming pool) x 6 weeks; allow the warm, soapy water to run over the wound(s) but do not scrub them.  Refrain from putting lotions, oils, or ointments on the wounds. Keep the areas clean and dry.  
Refrain from lifting heavy weight (i.e. greater than the weight of a gallon of milk) or doing strenuous activity for 6 weeks; refrain from driving for the next 2 weeks or as long as taking narcotic pain medication.  You may slowly resume normal activity, as tolerated, thereafter.  Walk at least three times a day.  
Outpatient follow-up with in-person appointment in 2 weeks  
Monitor for fevers/chills and changes to wounds (redness, swelling, drainage).
