### N = Nursing / "PATIENT CARE"
"Fall Risk Precautions": Select and complete both "Click here to document Fall Risk Precautions" and "Click here to document Fall Risk Safety Measures".  By default, ALL of our vascular surgery patients are at risk for falls and should have safety measures instituted.

Risk Assessment/Precautions: Generally, "Moderate" or "High" for fall and skin risks for ambulatory and non-ambulatory vascular surgery patients, respectively. "Yes" for aspiration risk. "Yes" for Wanderer if ambulatory patient with dementia or delirium.

>Call physician if: 
  SBP <100mmHg or >140mmHg despite more than two doses of PRN antihypertensives
  DBP >100mmHg
  pulse <55 or >120
  temp >100.4F
  UOP N/A


Text order: "Keep overhead lights on and curtains open during daytime hours (7A-9PM)."

Text order: "Up to chair with assistance for at least three hours daily."

### L = Lab studies, plus imaging and other testing
>1. Complete metabolic profile (CMP)
>2. Complete blood count (CBC) with or without differential
>3. PT/INR and PTT.
>4. Type & screen.
>5. Consider brain naturetic peptide (BNP), procalcitonin, c-reactive protein (CRP), MRSA nasal swab and/or baseline urinalysis.

Please do NOT enter daily pan-lab orders for inpatients; generally, 2x/week is sufficient for patients with oral intake who are not on hemodialysis.

### Imaging:
Chest x-ray only if jugular or subclavian central venous catheter placed in the operating room.

### Under "Consults/Procedures":
12-lead EKG on ALL patients.

### I = IV fluids
Based on patients situation; generally, saline lock if eating, no sepsis or bleeding.

### S = Specialist consults
None by default.
