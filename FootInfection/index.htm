MEDVAMC Management of Foot Infections & Gangrene

 

Tenets of Treatment:
  1. Deep infections get drained/incised.
  2. Ensure or establish adequate arterial inflow.
  3. Reconstruct, reestablishing intact epithelium over the entire extremity.
  4. Maintain mobility through the avoidance of amputation.

Example of a thoroughly-performed and effectively-communicated evaluation & plan:

Hi Dr. ___, Im calling about a man in the ER with a foot abscess that Id like to admit and operate on tomorrow. Hes 72 year-old man with diabetes. He tells me hes had an ulcer over his left 2nd metatarsal head for about 3 months. Hes been seeing a non-VA podiatrist for wound care. Over the past two days hes noted malaise, decreased appetite, swelling in his foot and ankle, and a new serous discharge from the ulcer. Denies chills or diaphoresis.

 

As I mentioned he has diabetes. His last A1C was 8.2%. He takes only metformin. He has a history of PCI with stenting after an MI three years ago, and he is one Plavix for this. He has stage 2 chronic kidney disease and hypertension. He used to smoke a pack a day but quit after his MI. He had ABIs done two years ago. The toe pressure on this affected left side was 45 [mmHg]. Hes ambulatory. He lives with his wife and is a retired office worker.

 

On exam he is alert and doesnt look sick. He did have tachycardia to 110 on one reading. No hypotension, no fever. I do see edema to the mid-calf. Hes got claw toe deformity of toes two through five. He has a ulcer under the second met[atarsal] head that is about 15mm in diameter. There is necrotic tissue at the base of the ulcer with serous drainage, and there is erythma surrounding the ulcer. Theres also a small sinus opening on the dorsal side of the base of the second toe that has serous drainage as well. The base of the toe feels fluctuant, and it feels like its got an abnormal degree of joint flexibility. I can feel the femoral and popliteal pulses on this left side, but I cannot feel pedal pulses. The DP and PT sound biphasic on Doppler. He can passively dorsiflex his ankle at least 10 degrees past neutral.

 

His glucose was 493 on Accucheck. His other labs are pending. The ER ordered a CBC, CMP and blood cultures. I am going to add a type and screen, and a CRP, and an x-ray of left toes.

 

So basically, I think hes developed an abscess that is tracking from the ulcer, through the 2nd MTP joint and to the dorsum of the foot.

 

The ER gave him a dose of vancomycin. Id like to admit him to 5A for the insulin drip protocol and all the usual admission orders. Ill get him Id like to add him on for OR tomorrow for incision and drainage, possible second toe amputation.

Special circumstances:

 

hypotension (SBP<90): treat like SIRS as above along with: SICU admission; vancomycin + cefipime; LR 30mL/kg (~2L) influsion over 3 hours; norepinephrine if not responding.

 

either tachycardia alone or 2+ SIRS criteria present (temp>100.5, pulse>90/min, RR>20, PaCO2<32, WBC>12K or 10% bands): get blood cultures, serum lactate, ECG, brain naturetic peptide (BNP), CK/CKMB/troponins at admission. Repeat EKG and BNP on day 2. Consult cardiology or vascular medicine if any are abnormal.

 

hyperosmolar hyperglycemic syndrome (HHS): serum osmolality >320 mOsm/kg.

(Serum Osmolality = (2 x (Na + K)) + (BUN / 2.8) + (glucose / 18) . Presumed when glucose >600 with sodium >140 or when glucose >500 with sodium >145..

Admit to ICU. Get serum ketones. Give 0.5-1L NS and start insulin drip. q6h metabolic panels and replace K+.

 

severe hyperglycemia (glucose 300-499): admit to 5A for insulin drip; give 500mL NS over 24 hours.

 

 

 

 

Day #0: Operative Treatment of Infection

ICD-10 L01.91 or I70.261/262; CPTs 28002, 28003, 11043; discuss with STAFF before posting.

Hard requirements for OR requirements: Type & screen; consent; NPO (solids x6h, clear liquids x2h)

Objectives:

1.      Incision Penrose drain for all abscess tracts.

2.      Obtain fluid, soft tissue or bone for microbiology (NOT swabs)

3.      Removal of all necrotic tissue; minimize resection of viable / marginal tissue

4.      Irrigate with saline + cysto tubing (not pulse lavage).

5.      Dress with gentamicin-impregnated 4x4 gauze in deep areas, Xeroform over shallow ulcer. Wrap with 4 inch Webril + 4 ACE from forefoot to knee to control edema.

Post-op orders: Continue most admission orders; saline lock IV; add diabetic diet + Glucerna BID, Iodosorb (cadexomer iodine) ointment; Do NOT order labs for POD#1

 

 

Day #1: Boot, Blood flow, wound care.

1.      Prosthetics consults for DH walker + 4-point rollator walker + shower bench + wheelchair.

2.      Text order for wheelchair + escort to prosthetics department (2B-400)

3.      Change dressing, using either cadexomer iodine gel (Iodosorb)

4.      Continued empiric IV antibiotic dosing. Vanc trough (if applicable)

5.      Order POD #2 labs: BMP, CBC with diff, CRP, procalcitonin

6.      If palpable pedal pulses → vascular lab consult for ABIs, toe pressures; if NOT palpable →. vascular surgery consult to schedule angiogram +/- intervention.

7.      Tell patient to anticipate discharge home in 48 hours.

At the clinic visit (done at 1-2 weeks after discharge)

1.      Check final culture results; change antibiotics as needed.

2.      Remove any Penrose drains

3.      If no more clinical signs of infection: transition from Iodosorb to Santyl, possibly with negative pressure wound therapy dressings.

4.      Ensure there is a plan for delayed secondary closure, skin grafting, or secondary healing.

NOTE: Nylon sutures in all amputation sites should not be removed until 6 weeks after surgery. Antibiotics are continued through this date.
